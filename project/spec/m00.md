# M00: Customer Truthing And Go/No-Go

## Objective

Validate the commercial, operational, and retention assumptions required to make the product viable before deeper implementation work proceeds, with emphasis on whether a known single-company success pattern can become a repeatable product.

## User-Visible Outcome

Founders have written customer evidence showing whether the target market accepts the operating model, retention model, initial source pack, and reusable-product boundary.

## Required Capabilities

- present the BYOC deployment model clearly
- explain the raw versus derived retention contract
- define the initial source pack and first five scenarios
- articulate why the initial wedge is a technical security product rather than a full whole-person IRM suite
- test whether design partners want reusable product modules or bespoke context and workflows
- capture design partner decisions and objections in a reusable format

## Acceptance Criteria

- at least five design partners confirm vendor-run BYOC is acceptable
- at least five design partners accept long-lived derived state inside their cloud account
- the initial source pack is stable enough to support implementation
- the top detection scenarios materially overlap across customers
- the analyst workflow expectations are documented and consistent enough to drive `m01` and `m02`
- founders can state clearly which customer requirements are reusable product surface versus likely services drag

## Dependencies

- none

## Explicitly Out Of Scope

- production-grade source collectors
- customer-facing investigation workflow
- customer-specific behavioral models
- response controls
